I lived in an apartment on Bloomingdale avenue for a little longer than a year. My windows faced the defunct embankment. On a daily basis I walked along it or underneath it, interacting daily whether consciously or not. Its painted white omnipresence seemed always just a glance away. Stark and immovable, yet frequently vandalized, eroding, and impermanent. These images are an excerpt of works made while walking the length of the embankment. They are thoughts, reflections, and interactions with the structure as it bisects the ever-changing surrounding neighborhood, ever-changing in its own right.
