While vacationing in New York City I came upon the new linear elevated park called the High Line. After seeing the great things that could be done with the abandoned elevated train lines of New York I wanted to see what possibilities might exist for the Bloomingdale Trail to create a similar urban park. My photos document a tour of the Bloomingdale Trail starting from the concrete caverns of the expressway overpass at Ashland Avenue to the still active rail yard at Drake Avenue. Along the way I was treated to the sights and sounds of the diverse neighborhoods that could be served by this development. The richness and variety of the ever changing scene suggest that this project has the potential to be considerably more interesting than the High Line.
