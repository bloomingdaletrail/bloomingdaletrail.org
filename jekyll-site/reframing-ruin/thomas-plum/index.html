Ride Bye A regular bike commuter, since 2004 I’ve wound around and through the streets that punctuate the Bloomingdale freight line. Looking up and down I pass concrete pillars, bridges, lintiles, massive footers, wobbly sidewalks, ancient metal lights and steel railings that support and adorn the tracks above. Of interest to me are the visual intersections of past and present on the edges of the line. At street level both history, culture and infrastructure meet. Colorful murals, graffitti and peeled paint mix freely against angular concrete edges which wrestle with decayed waterfalls of stone and rusted metal. Pictures only mankind, light and time can create. One day I’ll ride above on the Bloomingdale Trail. Straight as the crow flies to the river, without diesel trucks or volcanic potholes. And down below a blank canvas will pass by. Fresh construction and the start of a new asthetic. A second marrage of images, industry and entropy.
