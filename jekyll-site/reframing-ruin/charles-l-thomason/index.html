Chicago’s history is the story of a dirty, industrial trading post that evolved into a hip, beautiful center of progress. In many ways, the Bloomingdale Trail—and the changes it is currently undergoing—represents a microcosm of this same urban transformation. A three mile stretch of land that long helped expedite industry in North America, but later became stagnant, is being revitalized into a public place of modern beauty and urban facility. With these five photographs, I sought to use cutting edge creative techniques to portray the more industrial characteristics that have been left behind by the Bloomingdale Trail’s past, together with the natural beauty that has emerged over the last several years. Using high- dynamic range photography, long exposure, and micro four-thirds technology, I hope to capture the Bloomingdale Trail’s present personality, its history, as well as a hint of its potential for the future.
