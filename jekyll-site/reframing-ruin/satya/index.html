A Whimsical Space. As you stroll by taking in the sun, you might come upon a metalworker’s thoughts wrought in iron or you might be arrested mid-run, struck by that interesting piece of wood or that pitted stone you never noticed until now. If you’re really lucky, the Bucktown parrots might fly by - taking you back to summer holiday skies as a kid back in the tropics. Oh look it’s the tamale man… A tune just sprung in my head! The Bloomingdale Trail would feature lots to engage the senses: walking/running/bike paths, event space for music, poetry, performance, a telescope, native plant gardens, benches or maybe grass to picnic on, community vegetable patches, a pop-up restaurant, a sundial… all manner of things to discover and be inspired by. The railway tracks would be left in at places, as will some of the wilderness and track ballast. Development would be via sustainable methods and materials, with an intention of conserving and showcasing the Trail’s, Chicago’s, the adjoining neighborhoods’ histories. Development would preserve by design the edgy organic feel of the Trail, and would lend an air of exploration, mystery and discovery by placing works of art – visual, sound, tactile - at strategic unexpected points.
