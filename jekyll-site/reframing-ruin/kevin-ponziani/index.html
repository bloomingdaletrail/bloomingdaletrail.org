As a resident of the neighborhood where the Bloomingdale Rail passes through, I attempt to capture the architecture of the line as a part of the neighborhood. The Bloomingdale Trail, with its history and style, is just as much a part of the Wicker Park, Bucktown, Humboldt Park, and Logan Square neighborhoods and culture as the residents, architecture, and local establishments that call them home. My goal is to capture the trail, as it exists today, to be remembered by future generations before it becomes converted to a multi-use park. The still-vibrant colors and industrial style of the trail evoke a feeling of history come alive; helping to define the culture and feeling of the neighborhood it passes through. My personal fascination with the industrial urban architecture shows in my photographs. The photos for me evoke the imagination of what the rail line once was, an industrial fixture of the city, and what it can be, a repurposed park to be enjoyed by the residents who have replaced the trains who once traversed the neighborhoods it passes through.
